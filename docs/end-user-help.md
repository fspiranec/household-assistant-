# Household Finance Tracker User Help

## A. ABOUT THE APP

### Short version
Household Finance Tracker helps people record, organize, and review shared household spending in one place. It is designed for households, couples, families, or roommates who want a simple way to log expenses, tag them, keep some items private when needed, invite members, and review spending trends with charts and downloadable reports.

### Medium version
Household Finance Tracker is a shared expense tracking app for people who manage spending together. It is useful for couples, families, roommates, or anyone who wants a clearer picture of where household money is going.

The app lets you create or join a household, add expenses, organize them with categories and tags, and review spending through filters, charts, and summaries. You can mark some expenses as private, so they are stored separately from general household items when needed. You can also invite other people to join your household, edit your profile, and download the current year’s expenses as CSV or Excel files.

One of the main benefits is that the app combines everyday expense entry with household collaboration. Instead of tracking spending in scattered notes or spreadsheets, users can keep records in one place, see trends over time, and make better decisions about shared spending.

## B. QUICK START GUIDE

1. **Create your account** on the Register page.
2. **Log in** with your email and password.
3. **Create a household** on the Households page if you are starting from scratch.
4. **Invite other members** if you want to track spending together.
5. **Add your first expense** from the Expenses page by clicking **Add Expense**.
6. **Choose the correct household** before saving an expense.
7. **Enter the amount, date, merchant, category, and any tags or notes**.
8. **Optional:** upload a receipt image or file to auto-fill some fields.
9. **Open the Dashboard** to review totals, trends, and spending breakdowns.
10. **Use Export** when you want a downloadable copy of this year’s expenses.

## C. FULL HELP / USER GUIDE

### What the app does
Household Finance Tracker helps you:
- keep a record of expenses,
- organize expenses by household,
- separate private and shared spending,
- label expenses with categories and tags,
- invite other household members,
- review spending patterns with charts and summaries,
- export expense data for reporting or offline review.

### Who it is for
- Couples managing shared expenses.
- Families tracking household spending.
- Roommates splitting or reviewing common costs.
- Individual users who want personal expense tracking with the option to collaborate later.

### Navigation overview
After logging in, the top navigation shows these main areas:

- **Dashboard** – analytics, filters, totals, and charts.
- **Expenses** – list of expenses and link to add a new one.
- **Households** – create a household, view membership, add users, and create invite links.
- **Export** – download CSV or XLSX reports.
- **Profile** – update your personal details, email, and password.
- **Logout** – sign out of the app.

### Main features

#### 1. Account access
- Register a new account.
- Log in with email and password.
- Request a password recovery email.
- **Needs confirmation:** Google sign-in exists in the backend, but there is no visible Google sign-in option in the current user interface.

#### 2. Household management
- Create a new household.
- See your role in each household.
- Owners can view the full household member list.
- Owners can create a share link.
- Owners can directly add an existing user by email.
- Invited users can join through an invite link.

#### 3. Expense tracking
- Add expenses with amount, date, merchant, category, notes, and tags.
- Mark an expense as **private** or **household**.
- Reuse existing merchants, categories, notes, and tags through suggestions.
- Edit or delete an expense **only if you created it**.
- Filter the expense list by household, date range, category, tag, merchant, and private/public view.

#### 4. Receipt parsing
- Upload a receipt file when creating a new expense.
- The app may auto-fill merchant, total, and date from the receipt.
- **Needs confirmation:** the accuracy and supported file types are not clearly explained in the current UI.

#### 5. Dashboard and analytics
- Filter expenses by household, date range, user, category, tag, merchant, and private/public setting.
- View a spending breakdown by category, merchant, user, or tag.
- View trends by day, week, or month.
- Switch trend metric between total amount, count, or average expense.
- Review a list of all expenses currently included in the dashboard filters.
- See a “By user” summary for the filtered results.

#### 6. Export and reports
- Download the current year’s expenses as:
  - CSV
  - XLSX / Excel
- Use exported files for reporting, sharing, or spreadsheet work.
- **Important:** exports appear to include the current calendar year only.

## Major workflows

### Workflow: Create an account
1. Open the **Register** page.
2. Enter your username, first name, last name, email, and password.
3. Select **Register**.
4. If registration is successful, follow any confirmation steps shown by the app or your email provider if applicable.
5. Go to **Login** and sign in.

### Workflow: Log in
1. Open the **Login** page.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Select **Login**.
4. You will be taken to the dashboard after a successful sign-in.

### Workflow: Recover your password
1. Open **Password Recovery**.
2. Enter your email address.
3. Select **Send Recovery Link**.
4. Check your email and follow the recovery instructions.
5. After recovery, review your details on the **Profile** page.

### Workflow: Create your first household
1. Open **Households**.
2. Find the **Create Household** section.
3. Enter a household name.
4. Select **Create**.
5. Your new household should now appear in your household list.

### Workflow: Invite someone to your household
1. Open **Households**.
2. Go to **Invite Member**.
3. Choose the household.
4. Enter the person’s email.
5. Select **Create Invite Link**.
6. Copy the invite link or use **Open email draft with invite link** if shown.
7. Ask the person to open the link, log in or register, and complete the join process.

### Workflow: Generate a share link without entering an email
1. Open **Households**.
2. In a household card that you own, select **Generate Share Link**.
3. Copy the generated invite link.
4. Send it using your preferred messaging app or email.

### Workflow: Add an existing user directly
Use this only when the person already has an account.

1. Open **Households**.
2. Go to **Add User to Household**.
3. Choose the household.
4. Enter the existing user’s email.
5. Select **Add User**.
6. If the app says the user was not found, use **Invite Member** instead.

### Workflow: Join a household from an invite
1. Open the invite link.
2. If you are not logged in, choose **Log In to Join** or **Register**.
3. After signing in, return to the invite flow if needed.
4. Wait while the app checks the invite.
5. When successful, you will be redirected to the **Households** page.

### Workflow: Add an expense manually
1. Open **Expenses**.
2. Select **Add Expense**.
3. Choose the household.
4. Enter the **Amount**.
5. Select the **Date**.
6. Enter the **Merchant**.
7. Enter or choose a **Category**.
8. Choose whether the expense is **Private**.
9. Select an existing tag if you want one.
10. Add extra tags in the **Additional tags** field, separated by commas.
11. Add notes if helpful.
12. Select **Save Expense**.
13. Look for the confirmation message **Expense saved**.

### Workflow: Add an expense from a receipt
1. Open **Add Expense**.
2. Choose the household first.
3. Upload the receipt file.
4. Wait for the app to fill in merchant, amount, or date.
5. Review every field carefully.
6. Add or correct the category, tags, privacy setting, and notes.
7. Save the expense.

### Workflow: Edit an expense
1. Open **Expenses**.
2. Select the expense by clicking its date.
3. If you created the expense, the form fields should be editable.
4. Update the fields you want to change.
5. Select **Save Changes**.
6. Look for the message **Expense updated**.

### Workflow: Delete an expense
1. Open the expense detail page.
2. Select **Delete Expense**.
3. Confirm the deletion when prompted.
4. You will be returned to the Expenses list.

### Workflow: Find expenses
1. Open **Expenses**.
2. Choose the household.
3. Narrow the list with any of these filters:
   - start date,
   - end date,
   - category,
   - tag,
   - merchant,
   - include or exclude your private expenses.
4. Review the table of matching results.
5. Select an expense date to open full details.

### Workflow: Use the dashboard
1. Open **Dashboard**.
2. Choose the household.
3. Pick a quick date range such as **Today**, **This week**, **This month**, or **This year**.
4. For more control, use **Specific day** or **Custom**.
5. Decide whether to include or exclude private expenses.
6. Optionally narrow results by users, categories, tags, and merchants.
7. Select **Apply filters**.
8. Review:
   - the total amount,
   - the number of matching expenses,
   - the breakdown chart,
   - the trend chart,
   - the included expense list,
   - the by-user summary.

### Workflow: Download reports
1. Open **Export**.
2. Select **Download CSV** or **Download XLSX**.
3. Save the file to your device.
4. Open it in your spreadsheet app if needed.

- In **Tags**, choose one or more tags.
- Select **Apply filters**.

### Hide private expenses from summaries
- In **Dashboard** or **Expenses**, change the privacy filter to exclude private items.

### Reuse old categories, merchants, tags, or notes
- When entering an expense, start typing or select from the suggested options.

## Tips and best practices

- **Create a household before adding expenses.** New expenses need a household.
- **Choose a clear category name early.** This keeps reports easier to read.
- **Use tags consistently.** For example: `groceries`, `kids`, `utilities`, `travel`.
- **Review receipt-filled fields before saving.** Automatic parsing can save time, but it may not always be exact.
- **Use private expenses carefully.** They can change what appears in shared views and reports.
- **Add notes for unusual purchases.** This helps later when reviewing spending.
- **Use the dashboard filters before exporting.** The export page itself does not offer visible filters, so use dashboard review first if you need to understand what should be in your report.

## Troubleshooting / FAQ

### I cannot add an expense because no household is available.
You probably have not created or joined a household yet. Go to **Households**, create one, or join one through an invite link.

### I can open an expense, but I cannot edit it.
Only the person who created an expense can edit or delete it.

### My invite link does not work.
Possible causes:
- the link is incomplete,
- the invite was already used,
- the invite is no longer valid,
- you are not logged in.

Try logging in first, then open the invite link again.

### I uploaded a receipt, but some fields are wrong.
Review and correct the amount, date, and merchant before saving. Receipt parsing should be treated as a helper, not a final answer.

### I added an existing user by email, but it failed.
The direct add option only works if that person already has an account. If they do not, use **Invite Member** instead.

### Why do I not see the household member list?
The member list is visible only to the household owner on the Households page.

### Why is my expense missing from a chart or list?
Check:
- household selection,
- date range,
- user filters,
- category/tag/merchant filters,
- private expense filter.

### What is included in exports?
The app appears to export expenses from the current calendar year. If you need a different date range, this area may need enhancement.

## Common mistakes and how to fix them

### Mistake: Saving an expense under the wrong household
**Fix:** Open the expense detail page and change the household if you are the creator.

### Mistake: Forgetting to apply dashboard filters
**Fix:** After changing filters on the dashboard, select **Apply filters**.

### Mistake: Entering multiple tags in the single tag selector
**Fix:** Use **Additional tags (comma separated)** for more than one extra tag.

### Mistake: Expecting another user to edit your expense
**Fix:** The current app only allows the creator to edit or delete that expense.

### Mistake: Thinking “private” means hidden everywhere
**Fix:** Private expenses can still appear depending on the selected filters. Check whether your view is set to include or exclude private expenses.

## E. SUPPORT CONTENT

## FAQ

### What is the difference between a household expense and a private expense?
A household expense is part of shared tracking. A private expense is marked separately and can be included or excluded depending on the filters you choose.

### Can anyone edit an expense?
No. Only the person who created the expense can edit or delete it.

### Do I need a household before adding expenses?
Yes. Expenses belong to a household.

### Can I invite someone who does not have an account yet?
Yes. Create an invite link and have them register or log in from that link.

### What if the person already has an account?
If you are the household owner, you can add them directly by email from the Households page.

### Can I see who else is in my household?
Owners can see the full member list. Non-owners currently see limited member information.

### Can I upload a receipt instead of typing everything?
Yes. The app can try to fill in some fields from a receipt, but you should review the results before saving.

### What report formats can I download?
CSV and XLSX.

### Can I export a custom date range?
Not from the visible export screen right now. The current interface appears to download the current year only.

### Why are my dashboard totals different from what I expected?
Dashboard totals depend on your selected filters, including household, date range, users, tags, merchants, categories, and private expense settings.
